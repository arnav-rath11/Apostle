# Apostle AI

Apostle AI is an intelligent assistant designed to help users explore and map knowledge, specifically tailored for Wikipedia-based topic discovery. It connects complex concepts, explains them simply, and suggests related ideas to cultivate a deeper curiosity and understanding.

## 🚀 Status
Apostle AI is **fully functional** and ready to help you map your knowledge.

## ✨ The Apostle Experience: How it Works

### Information Retrieval
Simply ask Apostle AI about any topic. Whether it's "Quantum Computing" or "The History of Jazz," Apostle delivers concise, accurate explanations derived from expansive knowledge bases.

### The Knowledge Mapper
Apostle doesn't just answer; it connects. 
*Imagine this:*
1. **You ask**: "What is the Fibonacci Sequence?"
2. **Apostle explains**: Briefly covers the mathematical concept and its prevalence in nature.
3. **The Mapper**: Apostle automatically suggests related topics to expand your map, such as:
   - "Golden Ratio"
   - "Fractals in Nature"
   - "Phyllotaxis"

Apostle acts as your navigational compass, ensuring you don't just find a fact, but discover the fascinating threads that tie it to the wider world.

## 🔮 Future Add-ons
- **Apostle AI Image Processing**: Enhance Apostle AI to efficiently process and understand images, adding visual context to knowledge mapping.
- **Wikipedia Account Integration**: Connect your personal Wikipedia account to Apostle to personalize your journey, track read articles, and contribute to Wikipedia directly from the app.
- **Visual Mapping UI**: Implement interactive node-based maps to visualize the connections between topics.

## 🛠️ Technical Overview
- **Frontend**: React, Tailwind CSS, Framer Motion for smooth transitions.
- **Backend**: Node.js & Express.
- **AI Model**: The app uses the `meta-llama/llama-3.1-8b-instruct` model via the OpenRouter API for intelligent LLM processing and generating topic maps.

## 🏗️ Getting Started
To run this application, ensure you have your `OPENROUTER_API_KEY` configured in the backend environment variables.
